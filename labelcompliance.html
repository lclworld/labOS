Is your cosmetic label compliant?

This tool checks your product label against Ghana FDA labelling requirements for cosmetics (FDA/MCH/CHC/GL-LCH/2013/03). Work through each section, paste your claims, and get a clear picture of what needs attention before you submit for registration.

Section 01 Product Identity
First-time registrations will receive an FDA registration number after approval. You do not need it now — but it must appear on your label before the product goes to market.
Does your product name closely resemble any already registered product? A name that looks or sounds like a registered product will be rejected by Ghana FDA.
Section 02 Ingredient Declaration
Are all ingredients listed on your label?
Are your ingredients written in INCI nomenclature? INCI names are the internationally standardised names for cosmetic ingredients. E.g. "Aqua" not "Water", "Butyrospermum Parkii Butter" not "Shea Butter".
Are ingredients listed in descending order of concentration? The ingredient present in the highest amount must appear first, down to the least.
Does each active ingredient show its concentration or amount?
Does your formula contain any excipients known to be a safety concern? E.g. parabens, formaldehyde donors, hydroquinone, mercury compounds.
Are these safety-concern excipients declared on your label?
Section 03 Manufacturer Details
Is the manufacturer's name on the label?
Is the manufacturer's postal address on the label?
Is the manufacturer's physical (premises) address on the label?
Section 04 Distributor Details
Is the distributor's name on the label?
Is the distributor's postal address on the label?
Is the distributor's physical (premises) address on the label?
Section 05 Dates & Batch Number
Is the date of manufacture stated on your label?
Is the best before or expiry date stated?
Is a batch number present on the label?
Section 06 Net Content
Is the net content clearly stated on your label? E.g. 50ml, 100g, 30ml.
Section 07 Directions, Warnings & Storage
Have you included directions for use?
Are necessary warnings or precautions included?
Are special storage conditions stated? E.g. store below 25°C, keep away from direct sunlight, refrigerate after opening.
Are handling precautions included where applicable? E.g. avoid contact with eyes, discontinue use if irritation occurs, patch test recommended.
Have you stated the indications, frequency, route and conditions of use? E.g. apply to face twice daily after cleansing, suitable for daily use on body and hands.
Section 08 Mandatory Statements
Does your label include "Keep out of reach of children"? Required on all cosmetic products — no exceptions.
Does your label include "For external use only"? Required on all products intended for external use.
Does your label contain any religious scriptures, symbols, images or marks not directly related to the function or identity of your product? E.g. Bible verses, Quranic text, religious icons, unrelated imagery. These may be considered false or misleading claims under Ghana FDA guidelines.
Is your label in English, or does it include an English translation? If the original label is in another language, a full English translation must be provided.
